## RoleLogic — Best for Conditional Role Automation

[RoleLogic](/) is purpose-built for role automation using IF-THEN rules. It excels at scenarios where roles depend on other roles.

**Best for:**
- Verification cleanup (has Verified → remove Unverified)
- Tier-based reward systems
- Cross-server role synchronization
- Complex multi-condition rules

**Strengths:**
- [9 condition types](/concepts/conditions) including threshold-based conditions
- [Visual rule builder](/features/rule-builder) — no coding or commands needed
- [Testing sandbox](/features/testing-sandbox) — test rules before going live
- [Cross-server support](/features/cross-guild) — manage roles across multiple servers
- Minimal permissions (Manage Roles only)

**Limitations:**
- Focused exclusively on role management — no moderation, music, or leveling
- Free plan limited to 2 rules per server

**Pricing:** Free (2 rules) | Premium starts with more rules, up to 210/server. See [Plans](/plans).

## MEE6 — Best for Level-Based Roles

MEE6 is one of the most popular general-purpose bots. Its role management is tied to its leveling system.

**Best for:**
- Assigning roles based on XP/level milestones
- Servers that want an all-in-one bot

**Strengths:**
- Large feature set (moderation, music, leveling, custom commands)
- Well-known with extensive documentation
- Auto-role on join

**Limitations:**
- Role automation is limited to level milestones — no conditional logic
- Cannot create IF-THEN rules based on role combinations
- No testing sandbox
- Premium features are expensive
- Requires many permissions beyond role management

## Carl-bot — Best for Reaction Roles

Carl-bot excels at reaction roles — letting members self-assign roles by clicking reactions on messages.

**Best for:**
- Self-service role selection menus
- Color role pickers
- Region/interest role assignment

**Strengths:**
- Powerful reaction role system
- Supports buttons and dropdown menus for role selection
- Good moderation features
- Generous free tier

**Limitations:**
- No conditional role automation (IF member has X, THEN do Y)
- Reaction roles are user-initiated, not automatic
- No cross-server role management
- No testing sandbox for rules

## Dyno — Best for Basic Auto-Roles

Dyno offers basic auto-role assignment on member join, plus moderation tools.

**Best for:**
- Assigning a default role when someone joins
- Simple moderation + role combos

**Strengths:**
- Easy auto-role on join
- Comprehensive moderation suite
- Dashboard for configuration

**Limitations:**
- Auto-roles are limited to join events — no conditional logic
- Cannot react to role changes
- No cross-server support
- No rule testing
